Malaysian Costume


Malaysian Costume


Malaysia, a cultural nation with three largest races groups, which are Malay, Chinese and Indian. The diversity of races and religion had created a lot of different traditional costume.

Most of the Chinese traditional costume are red because they believe that red is auspicious.

The picture on the left side is called "Tang Suit", which is a Chinese man traditional costume. "Tang Suit" is a kind of top with collar and belly knot. It is mostly embroidery with floral pattern.

The picture on the right side is called "CheongSam" or "Qipao", which is a Chinese woman traditional costume. The word "CheongSam" is a Cantonese word and it represent a meaning of long dresses. The costume is made from silk with a collar and has buckles at the waist.

The picture on the left side is called "Baju Melayu", which is a Malay man traditional costume. It is a long-sleeved shirt that worn over trousers. Often with a short sarong called "Samping" which is worn around the hip.

The picture on the right side is called "Baju Kurung", which is a Malay woman traditional costume. It consist of a knee-length long-sleeved skirt called a "Kain". It usually worn with a scarf on the head.

The picture on the left side is called "Jippa", which is an Indian man traditional costume. It is a long jacket and the length ends just below the knee. It has a Nehru collar(a collar that stands up).

The picture on the right side is called "Sari" or "Saree", which is an Indian woman traditional costume. It is an unstitched strip of cloth ranging in length from four to nine meters that is draped over body. Shirts can be backless or halter-neck.
